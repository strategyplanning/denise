A note from Denise

You don't have to have it figured out to take the next step.

You may be here because you want something to shift — because what you've been doing isn't working any more, or because you can't keep going the way you feel right now.

Therapy, to me, is about being human. It's a place where you don't have to have things figured out or present a polished version of yourself. Together, we work to make sense of experiences that can feel confusing or overwhelming on your own — in a way that feels real, respectful, and deeply human.

By understanding yourself more clearly, you can find new ways of moving through life that feel easier and more fulfilling. That's the work we do here.

— Denise Evans

Meet your therapist

A space to slow down, think clearly, and be met as you are.

Registered Social Worker · 16 years of clinical practice

I'm Denise Evans, a Registered Social Worker and passionate mental health therapist. I love what I do — and I'm constantly seeking new learning so I can best support the people who trust me with their stories.

I didn't set out to become a therapist. I started in outdoor education, where I saw first-hand how time outside brought out people's smiles, energy, and connection. I noticed I was drawn to the people who struggled with these things the most. That pulled me into a therapeutic boarding school, and eventually into my Masters in Social Work at the University of Toronto. Sixteen years later, I'm still learning — because the people I work with deserve nothing less.

"My favourite part of therapy is the moment someone realizes there's nothing wrong with them — and for the first time, sees that change is possible."

The approach

Relaxed. Focused.
Unmistakably human.

A session with me should feel gentle, warm, and surprisingly natural — almost like sitting down with a friend and your favourite warm drink. And yet there's clear direction underneath. You'll always know the work has purpose.

01 · Safety

A steady presence, not a performance.

You don't need to curate yourself here. When things feel hard or overwhelming, my role is to listen, understand, and help you make sense of what's happening — without ever dismissing or judging.

02 · Direction

Conversational, but deeply intentional.

Sessions are a space where you can speak freely, explore thoughts and emotions, and be guided with clarity and purpose. You leave knowing what we worked on and why.

03 · Lasting change

Not just relief, but flourishing.

My goal isn't only to resolve what hurts. It's to help you understand yourself more clearly and build a life that feels easier, more connected, and more aligned with who you actually are.

Evidence-based methods, tailored to you.

No two people need the same thing. I draw from a carefully built toolkit — trauma-focused, body-aware, and grounded in the science of human flourishing — and weave approaches together around what you need right now.

EMDR Eye Movement Desensitization & Reprocessing

A cutting-edge trauma therapy that helps your brain finish processing memories that got stuck. Highly effective for PTSD, anxiety, grief, phobias, and performance anxiety. Extended 90–120 minute sessions available for deeper work.

Trauma · Advanced training
Somatic Body-based Therapy

Trauma and emotion don't only live in the mind — they're held in the body, too. Through gentle awareness and body-based practices, we help the nervous system settle and find its way back to safety, balance, and ease.

Nervous system · Body-In-Mind training
Parts
Work Ego State Therapy

We all have different "parts" of ourselves — shaped by our experiences and the ways we've learned to cope. With curiosity and compassion, we get to know them. Self-understanding, self-acceptance, and real flexibility follow.

Self-understanding · Compassion
DBT Dialectical Behaviour Therapy

Evidence-based skills for people who experience intense emotions, reactive moments, or relationship overwhelm. We build real tools — emotion regulation, distress tolerance, interpersonal effectiveness, and mindfulness — that you can use in the hard moments of a real life.

Skills · Regulation
EFFT Emotion-Focused Family Therapy

Built on the idea that caregivers play a powerful role in their child's emotional healing. Practical, research-supported skills for responding to your child's emotional needs — and for loosening the blocks that can get in the way of feeling fully effective as a parent.

Caregivers · Family system
APP Applied Positive Psychology

Grounded in the rigorous science of human flourishing — not toxic positivity. Through the Certificate in Applied Positive Psychology, I help you build on strengths, find meaning, and cultivate vitality. Healing what's wounded and creating room for what can flourish.

Flourishing · Strengths-based

Honouring land, identity & connection

All of you is welcome here.

I recognize that my work takes place on the traditional land of the Anishinaabe, Haudenosaunee, Wendat, and Mississaugas of the Credit First Nation, in the area now known as Georgetown. I also grew up along the shores of Lake Huron — the traditional land of the Attawandaron and Ojibway peoples. The land has long offered connection, grounding, and belonging, and I hold gratitude for that every day.

In my practice, I aim to bring that same sense of safety and respect into the therapy space. I'm mindful of the privileges I hold and the ways they shape my perspective and relationships.

All neurotypes, sexual orientations, gender identities, and lifestyles are welcome here. My hope is that everyone who enters this space feels seen, accepted, and supported as they are.
